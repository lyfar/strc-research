## What they found
Comprehensive review of how engineered nanosystems transduce focused ultrasound into localized bioelectric signals, mechanical stress, mechanoluminescent light emission, or controlled drug release for neuromodulation. Covers piezoelectric nanoparticles, mechanoluminescent probes, and sonosensitive drug carriers across rodent and primate models. Key finding: nanoscale transducers can convert ultrasound energy into multiple output modalities (electrical, optical, chemical) with spatial precision, though translational challenges around nanoparticle delivery, biocompatibility, and safety thresholds remain unresolved.

## Lateral connection
Bridges to STRC program through two paths: (1) the Calcium Oscillation Acoustic Therapy hypothesis (A-tier) could leverage nanosystem-mediated conversion of ultrasound to localized mechanical or electrical stimulation of outer hair cells, providing a non-genetic adjunctive route; (2) the Sonogenetic STRC hypothesis (C-tier) directly concerns ultrasound-mediated cellular modulation. The review catalogs the current state of ultrasound-to-signal transduction efficiency, which bounds what acoustic therapy could realistically deliver to the organ of Corti.

## Hypothesis suggested
Ultrasound-responsive piezoelectric nanoparticles delivered to the perilymph could generate localized electric fields mimicking OHC electromotility, partially compensating for lost stereocilin-mediated mechanical coupling. Testable via finite-element modeling of nanoparticle-generated fields in scala media geometry.

## What could be computed
FEM simulation of piezoelectric nanoparticle charge generation under typical cochlear ultrasound pressures (0.1-1 MPa) in a simplified scala media cross-section. Compare generated field magnitude to prestin-driven OHC voltage changes (~mV range). If within 1-2 orders of magnitude, the concept merits experimental follow-up.
