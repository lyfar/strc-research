## What they found
Autosomal recessive deafness 9, caused by OTOF gene mutations, is characterized by severe-to-complete congenital deafness1. Although gene therapy has shown benefits in a small number of patients2-5, its safety and efficacy across broader age ranges and longer follow-up periods, as well as predictors of treatment outcomes, remain unclear. In this single-arm, multicentre trial conducted at eight centres, 42 participants (aged 0.8-32.3&#x2009;years) received adeno-associated virus&#xa0;(AAV) serotype 1 carrying a human OTOF coding transgene (AAV1-hOTOF) at three vector dose groups, with up to 2.5-year follow-up. The primary end point was dose-limiting toxicity within 6&#x2009;weeks. The secondary end point assessed efficacy and adverse events. No dose-limiting toxicities were observed. Grade 3 adverse events included decreased neutrophil count. Hearing was recovered in 90% of participants treated with AAV1-hOTOF, with gradual and stable improvement in auditory brainstem response threshold from greater than 97&#x2009;&#xb1;&#x2009;1&#x2009;dB normalized hearing level at baseline to 54&#x2009;&#xb1;&#x2009;3, 51&#x2009;&#xb1;&#x2009;3, 50&#x2009;&#xb1;&#x2009;3 and 42&#x2009;&#xb1;&#x2009;5&#x2009;dB normalized hearing level at 1, 1.5, 2 and 2.5&#x2009;years, respectively, and behavioural audiometry improving from greater than 96&#x2009;&#xb1;&#x2009;3&#x2009;dB hearing level at baseline to 37&#x2009;&#xb1;&#x2009;5&#x2009;dB hearing level at 2.5&#x2009;years. Participants aged 0.5-18&#x2009;years showed greater hearing improvement than adults. A higher number of present distortion product otoacoustic emissions at baseline or biallelic non-truncated OTOF variants was associated with better hearing recovery. Participants with hearing recovery demonstrated gradual improvement in speech perception.
